## Department Overview

The Department of Physics at Xiamen University Malaysia (XMUM) develops physicists who can move comfortably between fundamental science, computation, experimentation, and industry-facing applications. Our programmes combine rigorous theory, laboratory training, student research, and international exposure so that graduates are prepared for postgraduate study, R&D work, and analytical careers beyond the traditional boundaries of physics.

Physics at XMUM sits within a young, research-active university environment in which students learn directly from faculty working in condensed matter physics, gravity, quantum information, photonics, nanoscience, particle physics, and semiconductor-related areas. The department's teaching and research culture emphasises depth, curiosity, and practical problem-solving.

## Message from the Head of Department

Professor Ong Chong Kim, Head of Department, frames XMUM Physics as a place where strong fundamentals and research habits are developed together. The department's direction is to give students a serious physics education while keeping them close to real research questions, contemporary instrumentation, and collaboration opportunities inside and outside Malaysia.

That approach shapes the department's small-cohort model: students are expected to ask questions, present work, write clearly, and progress from coursework into research projects, internships, and publication-quality output.

## Research Mission

Our mission is to advance physics knowledge while training graduates who can contribute in academia, high-technology industries, and interdisciplinary research environments. Current strengths across the faculty include:

- Quantum science, quantum information, and quantum biology
- Condensed matter physics, 2D materials, magnetism, and superconductivity
- Gravity, general relativity, and mathematical physics
- High-energy and particle physics, detector development, and neutrino research
- Photonics, laser-matter interaction, plasma processes, and optoelectronics
- Nanoscience, MEMS, semiconductor materials, and device fabrication
- Computational and statistical physics

## Facilities and Laboratory Environment

The department supports learning through a mix of teaching labs, project-based experimentation, and shared research environments. Facilities highlighted through the department's public materials and lab galleries include:

- Undergraduate Physics Lab I-IV teaching spaces for mechanics, thermodynamics, optics, electronics, and modern physics experiments
- Arduino-based guided design lab modules where students build and test their own measurement setups
- Shared laboratory environments supporting materials, photonics, semiconductor, and experimental physics work
- Seminar, poster presentation, and project discussion spaces that are used for student research communication

The department also makes active use of collaborations and external links, including research exchange opportunities with the University of Gdańsk and industry engagement relevant to semiconductor and technology development.

## Accreditation and Quality Assurance

The department announced MQA full accreditation for Physics on 1 August 2024, and XMUM publishes programme approval and accreditation references for its physics offerings on the official course pages. The current public listings include:

- Bachelor of Science in Physics (Honours): MQA/PA 12947
- Master of Science in Physics: MQA/PA 16346
- Doctor of Philosophy in Physics: MQA/PA 16345
- Master in Semiconductor Technology: MQA/PA 18101

Applicants should verify the latest status, intake dates, and entry requirements on the official XMUM pages before applying.
